Nuclear fusion is a process in which two or more atomic nuclei collide at a very high speed and join to form a new type of atomic nucleus. This process releases a large amount of energy.
Thermonuclear fusion involves the merging or "fusion" of atomic nuclei, facilitated by high temperatures that bring them close enough together for fusion to occur. These elevated temperatures turn matter into a plasma state. If this plasma is confined, fusion reactions can take place, driven by the extreme thermal kinetic energies of the particles colliding with each other.
